## Highlights from the release

- Employment **fell by 291 000 q/q** to 16.8 million — the first Q1 decline since 2021.
- Unemployment **rose by 237 000** to 8.2 million; unemployment rate **up 1.0 pp to 32.9%**.
- Formal-sector employment **fell by 245 000** — losses concentrated in Trade (−133 000), Community & social services (−98 000), Construction (−79 000), Mining (−41 000), Manufacturing (−40 000). Gains in Transport (+73 000), Finance (+43 000), Utilities (+30 000).
- Informal-sector employment **rose by 17 000** — gains in Community & social services (+54 000), Manufacturing (+41 000), Finance (+17 000).
- Youth (15–34) NEET rate remains elevated (see Figure 9 in the release).

## Comparison to Q3 2023

Two observations from comparing the two QLFS releases in this knowledge base:

1. The **strict unemployment rate** is volatile around 32% (31.9% in Q3 2023 → 32.9% in Q1 2025), with no clear downward trend.
2. **Manufacturing employment** ended Q1 2025 at ~1.68m, roughly **100–200k above** the Q3 2023 trough but still well below 2005 levels (~1.4m was the 2005 peak using a comparable definition — see [[mayekiso-trade-liberalisation-privatisation|Mayekiso & Lamla (2025)]]), consistent with the [[premature-deindustrialisation|premature-deindustrialisation]] thesis.
