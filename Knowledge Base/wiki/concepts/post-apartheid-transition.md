## Definition

The "post-apartheid transition" denotes the political, institutional, and economic shift from the apartheid regime to a democratic, constitutional order in South Africa. Politically, it begins with F. W. de Klerk's **1990** unbanning of the ANC and Mandela's release; formally, with the **April 1994** election of a Government of National Unity; constitutionally, with the **1996 Constitution**. Economically, the transition extends well beyond 1994 into the 2020s, because the redistribution promised by democracy is a slow-moving, contested process.

## Canonical periodisation (used throughout the knowledge base)

| Period | Political event | Economic event |
|---|---|---|
| 1985–1989 | State of emergency, capital flight | Apartheid-era sanctions (US CAAA 1986, EC sanctions) |
| 1990 | Unbanning of ANC, Mandela released | Beginnings of unilateral tariff liberalisation |
| 1990–1993 | CODESA, negotiated settlement | SA re-enters GATT, joins WTO |
| April 1994 | First democratic election | RDP adopted |
| 1995 | Constitution in draft | Ratification of GATT/WTO commitments |
| 1996 | Final Constitution | GEAR adopted, RDP Office closed |
| 1996–2000 | Mbeki succession | Tariff acceleration, exchange control liberalisation |
| 2000 | — | SARB adopts inflation targeting |
| 2008–2009 | — | Global financial crisis; mining super-cycle peaks |
| 2012 | Marikana massacre | State-owned enterprise crisis accelerates |
| 2014–2018 | Zuma-era state capture | Governance scores fall (WGI) |
| 2018 | Ramaphosa succeeds Zuma | "New Dawn" — limited reform |
| 2020–2021 | COVID-19, July 2021 unrest | Unemployment peaks at ~34% (QLFS) |
| 2024 | GNU with DA | — |

## Economic transitions

1. **Trade and capital account** — unilateral liberalisation from 1990, acceleration under GEAR, WTO schedule 1995–2000, TDCA with EU (1999), SADC FTA (2008).
2. **Labour market** — extension of labour rights (LRA 1995, BCEA 1997, EEA 1998, Skills Development Act 1998), but persistent high unemployment (see [[labour-market-south-africa]]).
3. **Fiscal** — move from apartheid-era debt to deficit reduction (GEAR), then counter-cyclical expansion post-2009, then fiscal fragility under Zuma, then debt stabilisation efforts from 2020.
4. **Monetary** — inflation targeting (2000), floating rand, occasional foreign-currency intervention; inflation generally within 3–6% band.
5. **State-owned enterprises** — partial privatisation (Telkom 1997, IPO 2003), corporatisation (Transnet, Eskom) without full unbundling; Eskom crisis from 2007 and beyond.

## Normative questions

- Did the transition deliver on the RDP's "better life for all"?
- How much of the disappointing post-1994 performance is attributable to GEAR's policy choices versus exogenous shocks (commodity prices, global financial crisis, COVID) versus structural legacies (apartheid capital stock, urban form, schooling)?
- What explains the persistence of 30%+ unemployment and a rising wealth Gini?
