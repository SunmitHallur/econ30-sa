## Headline aggregates (2023–24)

- **Value added at basic prices**: ~13% of GDP.
- **Formal employment**: ~1.4 million jobs (QLFS Q1 2024).
- **Exports**: ~40% of merchandise exports, concentrated in autos, chemicals, metals, machinery.
- **Real output**: roughly flat over 2008–2023, with load-shedding-driven contractions in 2022–2023.

## Sectoral composition

| Sub-sector | Share of manufacturing | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Basic metals & fabricated metals | ~15–17% | Steel mills (AMSA), aluminium (Hillside), fabricators |
| Chemicals & petroleum | ~20% | Sasol dominant; pharma is small |
| Motor vehicles & components | ~12–14% | APDP regime; big exports to EU |
| Food, beverages, tobacco | ~20–22% | Least tradable sub-sector |
| Machinery & equipment | ~8% | Mining and agri equipment |
| Electrical machinery | ~3% | Declining share |
| Textiles, clothing, leather, footwear | ~3% | Collapsed since 1990s |
| Wood, paper, printing | ~5% | Sappi, Mondi |
| Other | ~10% | Furniture, non-metallic mineral products |

Source: Stats SA P3041.2, SARB QB.

## Long-run trajectory

1. **1970s**: import-substitution peak; ~24% of GDP, ~1.9m jobs.
2. **1980s**: protection intensified by sanctions and exchange controls; employment plateau.
3. **1990s**: unilateral tariff cuts; employment erosion begins.
4. **2000s**: GEAR liberalisation consolidates; Chinese competition starts biting textiles; autos stabilise via APDP; ~1.7m jobs at peak.
5. **2008–2011**: GFC contraction; modest recovery.
6. **2012–2019**: stagnation; electricity and logistics constraints.
7. **2020–2023**: COVID, load-shedding, Transnet failures, July 2021 unrest.

## Why the sector matters beyond its share

- **Employment intensity** is higher than mining or finance; manufacturing jobs support about 3× as many dependents per job in poorer households.
- **Export base**: diversified manufacturing exports reduce terms-of-trade volatility compared to commodity-only exports.
- **Technological spillovers**: machinery, autos, and chemicals host most of SA's industrial R&D.
- **Political economy**: manufacturing unions (NUMSA) and sector bargaining councils are central to the Tripartite Alliance.

## Policy levers

- **APDP II** (automotive).
- **Clothing and Textile Competitiveness Programme** (CTCP).
- **Steel Master Plan** (2021).
- **Localisation** — PPPFA regulations 2017; updated 2022.
- **Green industrial policy** — renewables supply chain, EV strategy (white paper 2023–24).
- **Special Economic Zones** — tax incentives for designated zones.

## Analytical complications

- Official valuation of manufacturing under-counts contract manufacturing (often classified as "business services").
- Trade statistics classify some SA exports (autos, chemicals) as manufacturing even though their inputs (platinum, iron, coal) are commodities.
- Productivity numbers are sensitive to capacity-utilisation cycles and should be de-cycled before interpretation.
