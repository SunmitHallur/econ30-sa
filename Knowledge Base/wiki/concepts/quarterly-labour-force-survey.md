## What it is

The **Quarterly Labour Force Survey (QLFS)**, Statistical Release **P0211**, is the household survey run by Statistics South Africa that produces the authoritative labour-market estimates for persons 15–64 in SA. It replaced the Labour Force Survey (LFS, bi-annual) in 2008.

## Design

- **Frame**: Master Sample drawn from the Census 2011 (now transitioning to 2022 Master Sample).
- **Sample size**: ~30 000 dwelling units per quarter, drawn from a rotating panel.
- **Mode**: face-to-face interviews; since COVID, hybrid CATI/CAPI.
- **Rotation**: each dwelling unit is in sample for four consecutive quarters.
- **Reference period**: the calendar week preceding the interview (quarter).
- **Frequency**: quarterly; release lag ~6 weeks after quarter end.

## Key outputs (every release)

- **Labour-force status** (employed, unemployed strict, unemployed expanded, not economically active, discouraged work-seeker).
- **Unemployment rate** — strict and expanded — at national level and by province, age, sex, race, education.
- **Employment by industry** (SIC v5) — agriculture, mining, manufacturing, utilities, construction, trade, transport, finance, community/social/personal services, private households.
- **Employment by occupation** (ISCO-08 major groups).
- **Formal vs informal** employment (in non-agricultural employment).
- **Reasons for unemployment**, **job-search methods**, **duration** (annual supplements).
- **Youth in labour market** — breakouts for 15–24 and 25–34.

## Definitions (1998-onwards ILO-aligned)

- **Employed**: performed any work for pay, profit, or family gain during the reference week, or was temporarily absent.
- **Unemployed (strict)**: 15–64, not employed, **actively searched**, **available to start** within two weeks.
- **Unemployed (expanded)**: strict plus **discouraged work-seekers** (not searching but available).
- **Not economically active**: 15–64, not employed and not in the expanded category.
- **Labour-force participation rate**: labour force / population 15–64.
- **Absorption ratio**: employed / population 15–64.

Cross-reference: [[unemployment-definitions-south-africa]].

## Strengths

- **Comparability over time** (within post-2008 series), thanks to a stable questionnaire.
- **Disaggregation**: province × industry × occupation × demography cells are publicly released.
- **Methodological transparency**: sampling variability tables published with every release.
- **Quarterly**: timely enough to inform macroeconomic and policy commentary.

## Caveats

- QLFS is **not directly comparable** to the ILO-modelled WDI series — the latter applies further harmonisation and relies on periodic projections. Use QLFS for SA-specific work and WDI for cross-country comparison.
- **Informal-sector measurement** is partial — small undeclared businesses are systematically under-captured.
- **Revisions** are issued when population weights are updated (e.g. Census 2022 calibration introduced notable level shifts in 2023–2024 — see the release notes in [[stats-sa-qlfs-p0211-2023q3]] and [[stats-sa-qlfs-p0211-2025q1]]).
- Sample in deep rural areas and informal settlements has higher non-response; sampling-variability tables should be consulted for inference at provincial or industry level.

## Related Stats SA products

- **Labour Market Dynamics in South Africa (LMDSA, P0211.2)** — annual re-release of QLFS microdata with additional supplements.
- **Quarterly Employment Statistics (QES, P0277)** — establishment-side (formal employers) series, complementary to QLFS.

## Where the data live

- Statistical release PDFs: Stats SA website, P0211 series.
- Microdata: DataFirst (UCT) — requires registration.
- Aggregate time series: Stats SA InstiView tool; SARB QB online database.
